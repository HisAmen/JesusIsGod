Jesus Christ - The Living God ✝️

Original Date Written: 7/15/2025

Introduction

“Jesus answered and said unto them, Destroy this temple, and in three days I will raise it up.” — John 2:19

“That if thou shalt confess with thy mouth the Lord Jesus (kyrios), and shalt believe in thine heart that God (theos) hath raised him from the dead, thou shalt be saved." — Romans 10:9

Throughout this documentation you will notice how the names for God in Hebrew, especially in the Old Testament, are always inconsistent...

Old Testament Verses

Isaiah 6:1–10 – In the year that king Uzziah died I saw also the Lord (ăḏōnāy) sitting upon a throne, high and lifted up...

Isaiah 6:5 - Woe is me! for I am undone...
Isaiah 6:8 - Here am I; send me.

John 12:41 - These things said Esaias (Isaiah), when he saw his glory...

Isaiah 7:14 – Behold, a virgin shall conceive, and bear a son, and shall call his name Immanuel...

Isaiah 9:6 – For unto us a child is born... The mighty God (ēl)...

New Testament Verses

Matthew 25:31–32 – When the Son of man shall come in his glory...

Luke 2:11 – For unto you is born this day a Saviour, which is Christ (Kyrios) the Lord.

John 1:1–14 – In the beginning was the Word (logos/Jesus)...

Romans 10:9 – Confess with your mouth the Lord Jesus...

Titus 2:13 – Looking for that blessed hope, and the glorious appearing of the great God (theos) and our Saviour Jesus Christ...

Statements from Early Church Fathers

Ignatius of Antioch: "There is one Physician who is possessed both of flesh and spirit..."
Justin Martyr: "We will prove that we worship him reasonably; for we have learned that he is the Son of the true God himself..."
Theophilus of Antioch: "It is the attribute of God, of the most high and almighty and of the living God, not only to be everywhere..."

Research Notes & Remarks

In Jewish tradition, Adonai (אֲדֹנָי) is used instead of YHWH...

John 20:28 — Thomas explicitly calls Jesus both Lord (Kyrios) and God (Theos)...

Closing: It is very clear that Jesus Christ is being referenced both directly and indirectly as God...
